# VowNook — Vendor Outreach Kit

The marketplace is empty on day one — that's normal. Couples won't come until there are real
vendors to find. So the single highest-leverage thing right now is **hand-recruiting your first
cohort**. Goal: **30–50 published vendors in the GTA** before you spend a dollar on demand.

> **This is the whole job right now.** Not another feature. The kit below has existed for
> weeks; what has never happened is §8 Day 1–2 — building the list and sending the messages.
> Nothing else on the roadmap outranks it.

**What changed (2026-07-16) — your pitch is now much stronger.** The pages no longer wait on
vendors to become indexable: a city×category page indexes on **≥3 vendors OR a substantial
local guide**, and 52 guides are seeded, so **198 URLs are live in the sitemap today**. Inertia
SSR is on, so AI crawlers (GPTBot/ClaudeBot/PerplexityBot) can actually read them.

That kills the hardest objection in marketplace recruiting — *"why would I join an empty
marketplace?"* You are no longer selling a promise. A vendor gets a **free, SEO-optimised,
AI-readable profile on a site that already ranks for their city + category**, whether or not a
single couple has signed up yet. Lead with that.

---

## 1. Who to recruit first (and where to find them)

Concentrate on the **Greater Toronto Area** and the **high-intent categories couples search
for first**. Aim for ~5–8 real vendors per category so each city×category page can index.

| Priority | Category | Where to find them |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Photographers** | Instagram `#torontoweddingphotographer`, Google "Toronto wedding photographer", local FB groups |
| 1 | **Venues** | Google "Toronto wedding venue", banquet halls, estates, lofts; many have a marketing contact |
| 1 | **Florists** | Instagram `#torontoweddingflorist`, Google Maps "wedding florist Toronto" |
| 2 | **DJs / live music** | "Toronto wedding DJ", entertainment agencies |
| 2 | **Caterers** | "Toronto wedding catering" |
| 2 | **Planners** | "Toronto wedding planner" — they also bring *demand* (each manages many weddings) |

| 3 | Cakes / bakeries | "Toronto wedding cake" |

**Tip:** Planners are a force-multiplier — recruit a few early. One planner sources vendors
*and* brings couples.

## 2. The offer ("Founding Vendor")

Lead with what makes VowNook different from The Knot / WeddingWire:

- **Free to list. No monthly fee. No pay-for-leads.** You only pay when you **win a booking**
  through the platform: **8%** of the first $5,000, **5%** above that, **capped at $1,000**.
- **A free profile page that's already built to rank.** Their listing sits on live, indexed
  city×category pages (`/wedding-photographers/toronto`), with schema.org markup and
  server-rendered content that **ChatGPT, Claude and Perplexity can read and cite**. Most
  vendors have no idea AI assistants can't read their own JS-heavy Squarespace site. **This is
  value they receive on day one, with zero couples on the platform — lead with it.**
- **Founding Vendor status** for the first cohort: a **Verified + Founding badge** and
  **featured placement** at the top of search.
- **Every review is tied to a real booking** — no pay-to-play, no fake reviews. Honest social proof.
- **Fast-responder advantage** — the "responds in ~Xh" badge rewards quick replies with more bookings.

> One-liner: *"A free profile on pages that already rank for Ontario couples — and you only pay
> if you actually book a wedding."*

**Don't oversell.** They will ask how many couples are on it. Tell the truth: you're building
supply first, the traffic is early, and that's exactly why founding spots are free and
permanent. Vendors talk to each other — one inflated claim ends the whole channel.

## 3. Cold email template

> ### ⚠️ CASL — read before you send a single email
>
> Canada's anti-spam law governs this outreach, with penalties up to **$10M**. You are almost
> certainly fine, but only if you follow the rules:
>
> - **Implied consent** covers you when the vendor **conspicuously published** that business
>   email (on their own site / public listing), the message is **relevant to their business
>   role** (it is — you're offering a listing), and their page carries **no statement refusing
>   unsolicited email**. If a site says "no solicitations," **skip them.**
> - **Implied consent does not waive the form requirements.** Every message must identify you,
>   give a real mailing address + contact, and include a **working unsubscribe**.
> - **Honour opt-outs within 10 business days**, permanently. Keep the list.
> - **Scraped, guessed, or pattern-built addresses** (`info@{domain}` you invented) are **not**
>   conspicuously published. Don't send to an address you didn't actually see published.
> - Prefer their **web form or Instagram DM** when no email is published — a form submission
>   they solicited isn't a CEM problem.
>
> This is guidance, not legal advice. If you scale past a few hundred, get 30 minutes with a
> CASL lawyer.

**Subject:** Founding vendor spot on VowNook (free, Ontario couples)

> Ontario wedding vendors are a small, chatty community — being caught overstating your traffic
> gets you blacklisted in every FB group at once. Being the founder who was straight with them
> is a real advantage, and it's the only version that survives a vendor asking around.

## 6. Vendor FAQ (answer objections fast)

- **What does it cost?** Free to list. You only pay a success fee (8% to $5k, 5% above, max
  $1,000) **when a couple books you through VowNook**. Nothing for leads or inquiries.
- **Why join a brand-new platform?** You get a free profile on pages that are **already live and
  indexed** for your city + category, marked up so ChatGPT/Perplexity can read and cite it —
  that's worth something the day you join, before we send you a single couple. Plus a founding
  badge and featured placement, permanently, at no cost. Nothing to lose but 10 minutes.
- **How many couples are actually on it?** *(You will be asked this. Answer honestly.)* We're
  new and building the vendor side first — couple traffic is early. That's exactly why founding
  spots are free and permanent. Never quote a number you can't back up.
- **Are reviews real?** Yes — only couples with a confirmed booking can review, one per booking.
  No buying or removing reviews, ever.
- **Do I have to be exclusive?** No. List wherever you like.
- **How do payments work?** Couples pay deposit/balance through the platform (Stripe); your
  share is paid out to your connected account, minus the success fee.
- **How long to set up?** ~10 minutes: business info, a few photos, your packages.

## 7. How a vendor gets live (and how you approve them)

1. Vendor signs up at **vownook.com** → picks **"I'm a vendor"** → builds their profile
   (photos, services, pricing, optional video + brochure) → clicks **Submit for review**.
2. You (admin) go to **/admin/vendors** → review → **Approve** to publish.
3. Toggle their **Founding** and **Verified** badges from the same screen (for the first cohort).
4. They now appear on `/marketplace` and the relevant `/wedding-{category}/toronto` page.

> Reminder: each city×category page indexes once it has **≥3 published vendors** — so push to
> get at least 3 per category in your first push.

## 8. Week-one plan

- **Day 1–2:** Build a list of 60–80 GTA vendors across the priority categories (so 30–50 say yes).
- **Day 2–5:** Send the cold email / DM. Personalize the first line (reference their work).
- **Day 3–10:** Follow up. Offer to set up the profile on a call for anyone hesitant.
- **As they join:** Approve fast, grant founding badges, and aim for **3+ per category** so the
  SEO pages flip to indexable.
- **Then:** submit the sitemap in Google Search Console and let the programmatic pages compound.
