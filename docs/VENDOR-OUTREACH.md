# VowNook — Vendor Outreach Kit

The marketplace is empty on day one — that's normal. Couples won't come until there are
real vendors to find, and your SEO pages (`/wedding-photographers/toronto`, etc.) stay
`noindex` until each has **≥3 vendors**. So the single highest-leverage thing right now is
**hand-recruiting your first cohort**. Goal: **30–50 published vendors in the GTA** before
you spend a dollar on demand.

---

## 1. Who to recruit first (and where to find them)

Concentrate on the **Greater Toronto Area** and the **high-intent categories couples search
for first**. Aim for ~5–8 real vendors per category so each city×category page can index.

| Priority | Category | Where to find them |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Photographers** | Instagram `#torontoweddingphotographer`, Google "Toronto wedding photographer", local FB groups |
| 1 | **Venues** | Google "Toronto wedding venue", banquet halls, estates, lofts; many have a marketing contact |
| 1 | **Florists** | Instagram `#torontoweddingflorist`, Google Maps "wedding florist Toronto" |
| 2 | **DJs / live music** | "Toronto wedding DJ", entertainment agencies |
| 2 | **Caterers** | "Toronto wedding catering" |
| 2 | **Planners** | "Toronto wedding planner" — they also bring *demand* (each manages many weddings) |
| 3 | Hair & makeup | Instagram `#torontoweddingmakeup` |
| 3 | Cakes / bakeries | "Toronto wedding cake" |

**Tip:** Planners are a force-multiplier — recruit a few early. One planner sources vendors
*and* brings couples.

---

## 2. The offer ("Founding Vendor")

Lead with what makes VowNook different from The Knot / WeddingWire:

- **Free to list. No monthly fee. No pay-for-leads.** You only pay when you **win a booking**
  through the platform: **8%** of the first $5,000, **5%** above that, **capped at $1,000**.
- **Founding Vendor status** for the first cohort: a **Verified + Founding badge** and
  **featured placement** at the top of search.
- **Every review is tied to a real booking** — no pay-to-play, no fake reviews. Honest social proof.
- **Fast-responder advantage** — the "responds in ~Xh" badge rewards quick replies with more bookings.

> One-liner: *"List free, get found by Ontario couples, and only pay when you actually book a wedding."*

## 6. Vendor FAQ (answer objections fast)

- **What does it cost?** Free to list. You only pay a success fee (8% to $5k, 5% above, max
  $1,000) **when a couple books you through VowNook**. Nothing for leads or inquiries.
- **Why join a brand-new platform?** Founding badge + featured placement now, no upfront cost,
  and you're getting in before the SEO pages rank. Low risk, real upside.
- **Are reviews real?** Yes — only couples with a confirmed booking can review, one per booking.
  No buying or removing reviews, ever.
- **Do I have to be exclusive?** No. List wherever you like.
- **How do payments work?** Couples pay deposit/balance through the platform (Stripe); your
  share is paid out to your connected account, minus the success fee.
- **How long to set up?** ~10 minutes: business info, a few photos, your packages.

---

## 7. How a vendor gets live (and how you approve them)

1. Vendor signs up at **vownook.com** → picks **"I'm a vendor"** → builds their profile
   (photos, services, pricing, optional video + brochure) → clicks **Submit for review**.
2. You (admin) go to **/admin/vendors** → review → **Approve** to publish.
3. Toggle their **Founding** and **Verified** badges from the same screen (for the first cohort).
4. They now appear on `/marketplace` and the relevant `/wedding-{category}/toronto` page.

> Reminder: each city×category page indexes once it has **≥3 published vendors** — so push to
> get at least 3 per category in your first push.

---

## 8. Week-one plan

- **Day 1–2:** Build a list of 60–80 GTA vendors across the priority categories (so 30–50 say yes).
- **Day 2–5:** Send the cold email / DM. Personalize the first line (reference their work).
- **Day 3–10:** Follow up. Offer to set up the profile on a call for anyone hesitant.
- **As they join:** Approve fast, grant founding badges, and aim for **3+ per category** so the
  SEO pages flip to indexable.
- **Then:** submit the sitemap in Google Search Console and let the programmatic pages compound.
