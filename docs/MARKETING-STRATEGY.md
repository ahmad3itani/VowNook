## 0. The one-sentence strategy

**Win Ontario couples cheaply with free planning tools + compounding local content,
earn their trust with reviews tied to real bookings, and monetise through a
two-sided marketplace that gets more valuable with every wedding planned.**

VowNook doesn't have the classic marketplace cold-start problem: the **free
planning studio delivers value with zero vendors**, so couples are acquired by
the tools, and the marketplace monetises later. That single fact shapes everything.

---

## 1. The growth model (the flywheel)

```
        AI + programmatic CONTENT (SEO/blog/local pages, mostly automated)
                              │
                              ▼
   free PLANNING TOOLS  ──►  couples sign up (cheap acquisition, no paywall)
        ▲                     │
        │                     ▼
   better SEO + word    couples PLAN  ──►  they need VENDORS
   of mouth + fresh UGC        ▲                │
        ▲                      │                ▼
        │                  honest REVIEWS  ◄── BOOKINGS (revenue: commission)
        └──────────  trust + ranking signals ──┘
```

Two loops, connected by the booking:
- **Demand loop:** content → couples → planning → bookings → reviews → trust + SEO → more couples.
- **Supply loop:** more couples + real reviews → marketplace is worth more to vendors → more vendors → better matches → more couples convert.

**Everything in marketing should feed one of these loops.** If a tactic doesn't
add couples, vendors, bookings, reviews, or content, it's a distraction.

## 2. Positioning & brand

**Category:** the free, honest wedding-planning studio + vendor marketplace for Ontario.

**Differentiators (the messaging pillars — lead with these everywhere):**
1. **Free, genuinely** — plan the whole wedding (checklist, budget, seating, guest list, website, registry, AI planner) without paying.
2. **Honest reviews** — every review is tied to a real booking. No pay-to-play, no fake five-stars. *This is the moat and the PR story.*
3. **Local + intelligent** — Ontario vendors near you, plus AI (plan starter, honeymoon concierge) that does real work.

**Brand voice:** warm, editorial, practical, a little vulnerable (founder-led),
never spammy or salesy. Cream + gold, Fraunces/Playfair. Talk like a thoughtful
friend in the industry, not a SaaS.

**The enemy (every good brand has one):** pay-to-play vendor sites with reviews
you can't trust, and planning apps that paywall the basics. VowNook is the honest
alternative. Name it.

## 3. Audiences (ICPs)

### Couples (demand — the volume play)
- **Pain:** planning is overwhelming + expensive; vendor sites feel like a gamble.
- **Desire:** one organised place, free, with vendors they can actually trust.
- **Trigger:** just got engaged; starting to research costs/venues.
- **Objection:** "another wedding app?" → answer: *free, and the reviews are real.*
- **Where:** Pinterest, Instagram/TikTok, Reddit (r/weddingplanning), Ontario wedding FB groups, Google search.

### Vendors (supply — the monetisation + moat)
- **Pain:** lead-gen sites are pay-to-play and full of fake reviews; bad ROI.
- **Desire:** qualified local couples, fair terms, control of their listing.
- **Trigger:** slow season; frustration with The Knot/WeddingWire fees.
- **Objection:** "is anyone actually using this?" → answer: founding-member offer + real couples + you only pay when booked.
- **Where:** Instagram DMs, email, LinkedIn, local wedding networks, bridal shows.

### Planners (a channel *and* a segment)
- They bring multiple weddings + recommend vendors → a force multiplier.
- Sell them Planner HQ ($499/yr) **and** recruit them as a referral channel.

## 4. Funnel & the north-star metric

**North-star metric: weddings actively being planned** (couples who completed a
real planning action — guest list, budget, or website). It predicts bookings,
reviews, and revenue better than raw signups.

| Stage | What it means | Key metric | Lever |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Acquisition** | couple/vendor lands | organic sessions, signups | SEO/content, social, referrals |
| **Activation** | does a real action | % completing a planning action; vendor listing published | onboarding, AI plan starter, lifecycle nudges |
| **Revenue** | money moves | bookings, GMV, Atelier upgrades, planner subs | marketplace UX, Atelier value, commission |
| **Retention** | comes back | weekly active, milestone email opens | lifecycle emails, countdown, notifications |
| **Referral** | brings others | referrals sent, vendor invites | built-in referral loop, post-wedding flow |

The whole funnel is already instrumented (GA4 + Clarity + the admin console).

## 5. Channel strategy (in priority order for a bootstrapped solo founder)

### Tier 1 — Owned & compounding (where you win long-term, ~$0)
- **Programmatic local SEO** — Ontario city × category pages (already built + AI-enriched). This is the moat: density of useful local pages competitors can't easily match.
- **Blog / content engine** — the AI blog autopilot publishes weekly; you guide topics. Targets top-of-funnel ("wedding cost in Ontario", "free wedding planning").
- **GEO / AI-citation** — llms.txt + schema + answer-first content so ChatGPT/Perplexity/AI Overviews cite VowNook. (Enable SSR to unlock this fully.)
- **Lifecycle email** — the built-in welcome → onboarding → countdown → digest → post-wedding program. Retention + reactivation for free.
- **Referrals + promos** — the built-in referral loop + comp codes (FOUNDING50 today).

### Tier 2 — Earned & community (high trust, moderate effort)
- **Pinterest** — #1 wedding discovery engine; pins compound for years. Highest-ROI social channel for weddings.
- **Reddit + Facebook groups** — value-first participation (answer questions, mention VowNook sparingly). Never spam.
- **PR / story** — the "honest reviews tied to real bookings" angle is a genuine story for local press + wedding blogs. Founder-led, build-in-public on LinkedIn/X.
- **Partnerships** — venues (refer their couples), planners (channel), local directories.

### Tier 3 — Social reach (brand + top-of-funnel)
- **Instagram + Reels, TikTok** — short planning/budget content, repurposed from blog + the Marketing Studio.

### Tier 4 — Paid (only after the loops work; small, measured tests)
- Don't buy traffic until activation + retention are proven. Then test: Pinterest ads, Google Search ads on high-intent local terms ("wedding photographer {city}"), Meta retargeting. Cap spend; kill what doesn't pay back.

> **Supply recruitment is its own channel** — direct vendor outreach (DM/email),
> founding-vendor comp codes, bridal-show presence. Supply is the gating factor
> for marketplace revenue; treat it as a standing motion, not a one-time push.

## 6. The content & SEO engine (your unfair advantage)

You've built what most early startups can't afford: **content at scale, cheaply.**
- **Blog autopilot** + **local-SEO autofill** generate genuinely useful, on-brand,
  Ontario-specific content on a cadence — guard-railed so it stays Google-safe.
- **Programmatic city × category** pages give you hundreds of long-tail local
  landing pages.
- **The strategy:** own every "[vendor type] in [Ontario city]" and "[planning
  question] Ontario" query. Compounding, defensible, near-zero marginal cost.
- **Feed it with real signal:** real reviews + real vendor content + couples'
  activity make these pages better than pure-AI competitors. Keep the human layer.

**Rule:** AI scales the *draft*; you (or real users) add the *truth* (real reviews,
real vendors, real local knowledge). That blend is the moat — and keeps you clear
of Google's scaled-content penalties.

## 7. Two-sided playbook

### Supply side (recruit + retain vendors)
- **Founding cohort:** hand-pick 20–50 across metros/categories; comp code + featured badge + "founding vendor" status. (Mechanic already built.)
- **The pitch:** free to list, pay only on booking (8%), reviews tied to real bookings, real local couples.
- **Retain:** fast lead notifications, the "responds in ~Xh" badge, a clean dashboard, real bookings. A vendor who gets one booking becomes a believer + a referrer.
- **Make them market for you:** ask each to post "find us on VowNook" — their audience becomes yours.

### Demand side (acquire + activate couples)
- **Hook:** the free tools (low friction, immediate value). Lead with "free", not "marketplace".
- **Activate:** get them to a real action fast (AI plan starter does this). Lifecycle nudges if they stall.
- **Convert to marketplace:** once they're planning, surface relevant local vendors; the inquiry → quote → book flow monetises.

## 8. Retention & lifecycle (the cheapest growth there is)

Already built — make sure it's running (scheduler on):
- **Welcome → onboarding nudges → countdown milestones (100/30/7/1 days) → weekly digest.**
- **Guest RSVP reminders** (couple-triggered) — sticky, Joy's edge.
- **Referral rewards** + **post-wedding flow** (thank-you + review requests + a perk code).
- **In-app notifications** keep couples coming back.

Retention compounds: a couple plans for 6–18 months. Keep them engaged and they
book vendors, leave reviews, and refer friends — all inside the loop.

## 10. The 90-day roadmap

**Days 0–30 — Prove the loop (beta).**
- Founding vendors recruited (real supply). Demo vendors only until then.
- 5–15 couple testers via FOUNDING50. Fix what breaks.
- Content engine running (1 blog/week, local pages filling).
- Goal: a handful of real bookings + first real reviews. Validate activation.

**Days 30–60 — Density in 1–2 metros.**
- Go deep in Toronto + one more (Ottawa/Hamilton): recruit enough vendors per category to make the marketplace genuinely useful there.
- Turn on autopilots fully; submit sitemap; request indexing; enable SSR.
- Start Pinterest + community in earnest. First PR outreach (the honest-reviews story).

**Days 60–90 — Compound + expand.**
- Lean into whatever channel showed the best CAC/activation.
- Referral + post-wedding loops producing their first referred signups + reviews.
- Expand metros as supply allows. Consider first small paid test (Pinterest/Search).

## 11. Budget & resourcing (bootstrapped)

- **$0 channels do the heavy lifting:** SEO/content (automated), lifecycle email, referrals, community, founder-led social. This is ~80% of the plan.
- **Small spend, high leverage:** Canva Pro, a scheduler (Buffer/Metricool/Tailwind), maybe a bridal-show booth in a target metro.
- **Hold paid ads** until activation + retention are proven, then test with a hard cap and kill losers fast.
- **Your scarcest resource is time** — the AI content engine + lifecycle automation are there precisely to buy it back. Spend your hours on the two things that don't automate: **recruiting real vendors** and **talking to real couples**.

---

## 12. Defensibility (the moats you're building)

1. **Local SEO density** — hundreds of useful Ontario pages, compounding.
2. **Honest-reviews trust** — structurally hard for pay-to-play competitors to copy.
3. **Switching cost** — a couple's planning data (guests, budget, seating, website) lives in VowNook; they don't leave mid-plan.
4. **Two-sided network effects** — more vendors + reviews → more couples → more vendors.
5. **AI content scale at near-zero cost** — output a small team couldn't match.

---

## 13. Measurement cadence

- **Weekly:** signups, activation %, new vendors, inquiries→bookings, content shipped, top organic pages (Search Console).
- **Monthly:** GMV, Atelier/planner conversions, CAC by channel, retention cohort, reviews collected, referral rate.
- **The one number to watch:** **weddings actively being planned** (the north star). If it grows weekly, everything else follows.
