# VowNook — Launch Marketing Playbook

> Two-sided Ontario wedding marketplace: a **free** planning studio (checklist, budget, seating,
> guest list, wedding website, registry, AI assistant + honeymoon concierge) **plus** a curated
> marketplace of vetted Ontario vendors with **honest reviews tied to real bookings**. Vendors pay
> only on success (8%). Couples pay nothing to plan.

---

## 0. The core idea behind the plan

Most marketplaces die on the cold-start problem (no vendors → no couples → no vendors). **VowNook
doesn't.** The free planning studio is fully useful on day one with an empty marketplace, so:

- **Couples are acquired by the free tools** (low friction, immediate value, great SEO surface).
- **The marketplace is seeded separately** by recruiting *founding vendors* before/around launch.
- **Trust is the moat:** every review is tied to a real booking — say this everywhere.

So we run **two motions in parallel**: supply (recruit founding vendors) + demand (couples via
content/SEO/community), connected by the booking + review loop you already built.

---

## 1. Positioning & messaging

**One-liner (use everywhere):**
> Plan your whole wedding for free — and book trusted Ontario vendors you can actually believe in.

**Tagline options (pick one, use consistently):**
- *Everything for your wedding. Nothing for the planning.*
- *Plan it all in one place. For free.*
- *Honest vendors. Free planning. One Ontario wedding studio.*

**Three differentiators (lead with these):**
1. **Free, genuinely** — checklist, budget, seating chart, guest list, wedding website, registry, AI planner. No paywall to plan.
2. **Honest reviews** — every review is tied to a real booking, so you can trust them. No paid placements.
3. **Ontario-local + AI** — vendors near you, plus an AI honeymoon concierge that builds a real, bookable trip.

**Brand voice:** warm, editorial, helpful, never spammy or salesy. Cream + gold, Fraunces/Playfair
display type. Talk like a thoughtful friend who happens to know the wedding industry.

**Audiences:**
- **Couples** (demand) — pain: planning is overwhelming + expensive; vendors feel like a gamble.
- **Vendors** (supply) — pain: lead-gen sites are pay-to-play and full of fake reviews; they want real, qualified couples.
- **Planners** — pain: juggling clients in spreadsheets; want one HQ.

## 2. Launch timeline

### Phase 1 — Pre-launch (Weeks -4 to -1): seed supply + build a waitlist
- **Recruit 20–50 founding vendors** across the 7 Ontario metros (Toronto, Ottawa, Hamilton,
  London, Kitchener-Waterloo, Windsor, Mississauga) and the key categories (venues, photographers,
  planners first — they refer everyone else). Use the **founding-vendor** badge + a comp/featured
  code as the incentive (see §5, "what to use").
- **Stand up the brand profiles** (§3) and start posting value content (not "we're launching").
- **Open a couples waitlist / soft signups** — every couple who plans is a future booking.
- **Line up 3–5 launch blog posts** already seeded; publish 1/week to start ranking.

### Phase 2 — Launch week: make noise on demand
- **Day 1:** Launch announcement across all channels (§4). Email the waitlist + founding vendors.
- **Day 2–3:** Founding-vendor spotlights (their content = their audience sees VowNook).
- **Day 3:** Product Hunt launch (optional, drives early adopters + vendors).
- **Day 4–5:** Value posts (planning tips, budget breakdowns) → soft CTA to the free tools.
- **All week:** answer 3–5 Reddit/Facebook-group questions *helpfully* with a soft mention.

### Phase 3 — Post-launch (ongoing): compound the loops
- **Content engine:** 3–5 posts/week (Pinterest-heavy) + 1 blog/week. Use the Marketing Studio.
- **Referral loop:** couples invite couples (built-in) → comp reward.
- **Review loop:** post-wedding review requests (built-in) → honest reviews → trust + SEO.
- **Vendor flywheel:** each new vendor brings their own audience; ask for a "find me on VowNook" post.

## 5. What to use (tools)

### Tools you already built — use these first
- **VowNook Marketing Studio** (`C:\Users\ahmad\vownook-marketing`, local Next.js) — your on-brand
  AI content engine: 12 generators × 4 channels (SEO blog, Pinterest/IG/TikTok, community, outreach).
  **This is your scale lever** — draft the weekly batch here, review, schedule. Stage-by-default.
- **In-app marketing engine** — comp/promo codes (use for **founding-vendor incentives**), the
  **referral loop** (couples invite couples), **founding/featured badges** (vendor recruitment carrot),
  **lifecycle emails** + **post-wedding review requests** (the trust + retention flywheel).
- **Built-in SEO** — programmatic Ontario city×category pages + blog + schema + llms.txt. Just keep
  publishing and (post-deploy) **enable SSR** so AI crawlers can read your content.

### External tools (mostly free tiers)
- **Scheduling:** Metricool or Buffer (IG/FB/LinkedIn/TikTok) + **Tailwind or Later** for Pinterest.
- **Design:** Canva — build a cream/gold branded template kit (pins, carousels, stories) once, reuse.
- **Email marketing:** Resend is wired for lifecycle/transactional; for opt-in marketing broadcasts
  use the in-app broadcast or MailerLite (CASL-friendly double opt-in).
- **Analytics:** GA4 + Microsoft Clarity + Search Console (all wired — just add the keys + submit the sitemap).
- **Vendor research/outreach:** Instagram + Google Maps + local wedding directories to build the
  founding-vendor target list per metro.

### Compliance (Canada — don't skip)
- **CASL:** only email people who opted in; every marketing email needs sender identity + a working
  unsubscribe (already built). Keep consent timestamps.
- **No fake engagement / no mass-spam** — it's both against platform ToS and against the brand. The
  honest-reviews positioning only works if the marketing is honest too.

## 7. What to measure
- **Couples:** signups, % who complete a planning action (guest list / budget / website), referrals sent.
- **Vendors:** listings live, % published, inquiries received, first booking.
- **Marketplace:** inquiries → offers → bookings (the funnel), first reviews.
- **Top of funnel:** organic sessions (Search Console), Pinterest impressions, email open/click.
- **North-star for the loop:** **bookings** (they create reviews → trust → SEO → more couples).
