## 1. What VowNook is

**One line**
> A free wedding-planning studio and a marketplace of Ontario wedding vendors,
> where every review is tied to a real booking.

**Elevator (30 seconds)**
> VowNook is where Ontario couples plan their whole wedding for free — guest
> list, RSVPs, budget, seating chart, wedding website and registry — and then
> find and book vendors in the same place. Vendors list free and only pay when
> they actually win a booking, so nobody buys their way to the top and every
> review comes from a couple who really hired them.

**The promise, in the couple's words**
> *"Bring your budget. We'll make it work."*

**The three things that make us different** — in priority order, because these
are the only claims that survive scrutiny:

1. **The planning tools are genuinely free.** Not a trial. No card. Competitors
   gate the good parts (seating charts especially) behind a paywall.
2. **Reviews are tied to real bookings.** One review per confirmed booking.
   Vendors cannot pay to remove, reorder or fake them, and cannot pay for
   placement. The category norm is pay-to-play.
3. **Real Ontario pricing, city by city.** We publish what a wedding actually
   costs in 42 Ontario cities across 12 vendor categories. No competitor
   publishes this. It is our only genuinely un-copyable asset.

## 2. The business model (exact numbers)

| Who | What they pay |
|---|---|
| **Couples** | **$0.** Planning tools, browsing, quotes and booking are all free, forever, no card. |
| **Couples — Atelier** | **$99 one-time, per wedding.** Adds the wedding website, `name.vownook.com` address, registry, travel, save-the-dates, printables, floor plan and collaborators. Not a subscription. |
| **Planners — HQ** | **$499/year.** Unlimited client weddings, portfolio dashboard, reusable templates. |
| **Vendors** | **Free to list.** Success fee only when a booking is won: **8% of the first $5,000, 5% above that, capped at $1,000 per booking.** No monthly fee, no contract, no charge for leads. |

**Referral:** $20 off Atelier + Shop credit, double-sided.

**Payments:** Stripe Connect, live. Couples pay deposit/balance through the
platform; the vendor's share lands in their connected account minus the fee.

## 3. Who we're for

### ICP 1 — The couple (primary)
- Ontario, engaged, planning 8–18 months out. Skews 26–36.
- Usually one person does 80% of the research. That person is our user.
- **Their real problem is not "inspiration" — it's cost anxiety and vendor
  trust.** They do not know what things should cost, and they do not believe
  the reviews they read.
- **They find us** by searching costs ("how much does a wedding photographer
  cost in Ottawa"), vendors by city, or planning tools.
- **What converts them:** a straight answer about money, and free tools with no
  card.

### ICP 2 — The vendor (the constraint)
- Ontario wedding businesses: photographers, venues, planners, florists,
  caterers, DJs, HMUA, cake, officiants, transport, attire, video.
- Usually a solo operator or a small team. Often the owner does the marketing.
- **Their real problem:** paying monthly for directory listings and lead
  packages that produce tyre-kickers, and having no control over placement.
- **What converts them:** free listing, pay-only-on-success, and — right now —
  a free SEO'd profile on pages that already rank.

### ICP 3 — The planner (force multiplier)
- One planner brings vendors *and* couples. Disproportionately worth recruiting.

## 5. ⚠️ Claims policy — read this before writing any ad

This is the most important section in the document. VowNook is early. Ontario's
wedding-vendor community is small and talks constantly; one inflated claim ends
the channel. Canada's Competition Act also prohibits misleading advertising.

**We CAN say:**
- "Free wedding planning tools — no credit card."
- "Every review is tied to a real booking."
- "Vendors list free and pay only when they win a booking."
- "Real Ontario wedding costs, city by city."
- "Built Ontario-first."
- "Founding vendor spots are free and permanent."

**We CANNOT say (today):**
- ❌ Any vendor count, or "hundreds of vendors" — the marketplace currently
  carries **sample listings**, clearly labelled as such, plus whatever real
  vendors have signed. Never imply scale we don't have.
- ❌ Any couple/user count, testimonials, or "loved by couples."
- ❌ "Trusted by…", "#1", "the best", "leading" — unsubstantiated superlatives.
- ❌ Review counts or star averages until they exist from real bookings.
- ❌ Fabricated urgency ("only 3 spots left") unless literally true.

**Cost figures must always be framed as estimates**, never quotes — Competition
Act and Ontario Consumer Protection Act. The product does this; marketing must
too. Phrase as "typically" / "usually runs" / "estimates, not quotes."

**When a vendor asks how many couples are on the platform, tell the truth:**
we're building the vendor side first, traffic is early, and that's exactly why
founding spots are free and permanent. That answer wins more vendors than a
number would.

## 6. Brand voice

**We sound like:** a straight-talking, well-organised friend who happens to know
what things cost. Calm, warm, specific. Never breathless.

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Lead with the number or the answer | Bury it under a story |
| "Most Ontario couples pay $1,800–$9,000" | "Prices vary depending on many factors!" |
| Short sentences. Concrete nouns. | Stacked adjectives, "elevate", "seamless", "curated experience" |
| Canadian spelling — colour, favour, organised | US spelling |
| Admit what we don't have yet | Imply scale we lack |
| Em dashes and plain words | Exclamation marks (almost never) |

**Banned words:** effortless, seamless, magical, dream team, elevate, unlock
(as a verb for features), game-changing, revolutionary, "say I do to…"

**The house test:** would a smart, slightly sceptical bride forward this to her
fiancé without cringing? If not, rewrite.

## 7. Visual identity — "Botanical Press"

Redesigned 2026-07-19. The concept is a **letterpress wedding programme**, not a
luxury landing page. Deliberately avoids both the blush-pink wedding cliché and
the cream-and-gold look that reads as machine-made.

### Colour

| Role | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Limestone paper | `#f1f0ea` | Page ground |
| Forest ink | `#0f1c17` | Body text, dark sections |
| Deep forest | `#1b4638` | Brand — logo, links, accents |
| **Persimmon** | `#c4502e` | **Calls to action ONLY** |
| Sage | `#7fb79e` | Accents on dark |
| Section tint | `#e7e9e2` | Alternating bands |

> **The one rule:** persimmon is *only ever* a call to action. The moment it
> appears as decoration, the eye stops knowing where to go and conversion drops.
> Green is the brand colour; orange is the button.

### Type
- **Display:** Newsreader — variable serif, optical sizing, editorial italic.
- **Body/UI:** Instrument Sans.
- Prices and figures use **tabular numerals** so they align in columns.
- Always use typographic quotes (" ") and apostrophes (') in display copy.

### Layout signature
- **Nothing is centred.** Content sits on an offset grid with a left rail
  carrying a chapter number (N°01, N°02…) and a short italic label.
- Hairline rules, letterspaced uppercase eyebrows, generous space.
- Subtle paper grain over everything.

### Logo
- Forest green mark + "VowNook" wordmark in Newsreader.
- Never on a busy photo without a solid ground behind it.

## 12. Compliance (non-negotiable)

- **CASL** — every commercial email needs sender ID, a real mailing address and
  a working unsubscribe honoured within 10 business days. Implied consent from a
  *conspicuously published* business address is fine; guessed addresses are not.
  Penalties reach $10M. Details in `VENDOR-OUTREACH.md`.
- **Competition Act / Ontario CPA** — all cost figures are estimates, never
  quotes. No misleading claims. See §5.
- **PIPEDA** — analytics are consent-gated; no sensitive-attribute targeting.
- **Reviews** — never solicit, incentivise or edit a review. The booking-tied
  model is the brand; breaking it destroys the only real differentiator.

## 14. The honest position

Written down so marketing never drifts from reality:

VowNook is a **complete, well-built product with no marketplace liquidity yet.**
The software is real. The SEO engine is real. The cost data is real and
genuinely unique. What does not yet exist is a base of real vendors and any
booking revenue.

Therefore: **every marketing dollar and hour should go to vendor recruitment
until roughly 20–30 real vendors are live in the GTA.** Content and SEO compound
in the background at no marginal cost. Paid demand generation is premature and
would actively damage the brand by sending couples to empty category pages.

The single highest-value action available at any given moment is almost always:
*send the next vendor email.*
